A collection of the author's short stories, compiled from works published at various periods in Grabiński's career. I already knew some of the stories included here, while others were new to me. I'll briefly describe a few that are worth reading.

#### In a Villa by the Sea
Horror intertwines with crime. While visiting his friend at the titular villa by the sea, the protagonist stumbles upon a potential murder. Psychic abilities are in the background.

#### Passion (L'appassionata). A Venetian Tale
A very unassuming short story. At first, it seems to be a simple romance set in Venice. The characters' subsequent adventures reveal a darker and more supernatural side to their relationship. The plot is simple, but beautifully rendered in Polish.

#### The Grey Room
A gripping story with elements of parapsychology. The room's occupant struggles with a strange anxiety, possibly left by the previous occupant. The story unfolds in a dreamlike atmosphere and the protagonist's experiences, where truth intertwine with paranormal themes.
